In India, poverty remains a pressing issue, despite strides in economic growth. Recent reports highlight the persistent challenges faced by millions living below the poverty line. Rural areas continue to bear the brunt of poverty, with limited access to resources and opportunities. Lack of education and healthcare exacerbates the cycle of poverty for many families. Unemployment rates, particularly in rural regions, contribute to the prevalence of poverty. The COVID-19 pandemic has further deepened economic disparities, pushing more people into poverty. Daily wage earners and informal sector workers are among the worst affected by economic downturns. Women and children are disproportionately affected by poverty, facing higher rates of malnutrition and lack of access to education.



Latin America, Caribbean ‘must step up’ to tackle rising hunger: FAO

 

25 January 2023 | SDGs

Countries in Latin America and the Caribbean “can and must step up” to tackle rising levels of hunger, poverty and inequality, the UN Food and Agriculture Organization, FAO, said on Wednesday.

Government initiatives such as the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (NREGA) aim to alleviate poverty by providing employment opportunities. However, implementation challenges and corruption often hinder the effectiveness of such schemes. Poverty in urban areas also remains a significant concern, with slums and informal settlements housing a large portion of the population. Limited access to clean water and sanitation facilities further exacerbates living conditions in urban poverty. Education is often seen as a pathway out of poverty, but many children from impoverished backgrounds struggle to attend school regularly. Child labor remains prevalent in India, with many children forced to work in hazardous conditions to support their families. NGOs and grassroots organizations play a crucial role in providing support and resources to impoverished communities. Microfinance initiatives have helped empower women in rural areas by providing access to small loans for entrepreneurship.



Around 258 million need emergency food aid: UN-backed report

 

3 May 2023 | Humanitarian Aid

The number of people who need urgent food, nutrition and livelihood assistance rose for the fourth consecutive year in 2022, due to conflict, climatic change, and COVID-19 impacts, the UN and partners said in a report published on Wednesday.Climate change poses additional challenges to poverty alleviation efforts, particularly in agricultural communities vulnerable to droughts and floods. Lack of land ownership and access to markets limit the potential for economic growth in rural areas. Social stigmas and discrimination based on caste, ethnicity, and religion further marginalize vulnerable communities. The government's poverty alleviation programs often face criticism for their limited reach and effectiveness. Data shows that a significant portion of India's population still lives below the poverty line, struggling to meet basic needs. Food insecurity remains a widespread issue, with many families unable to afford nutritious meals. Health outcomes are poorer among the impoverished, with higher rates of infant mortality and malnutrition. Migration from rural to urban areas in search of livelihood opportunities is common among the impoverished, leading to overcrowding and strain on urban infrastructure.
